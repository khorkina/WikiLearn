🎨 Design Guidelines for WikiLearn (Inspired by Ko-fi)
The UI/UX of WikiLearn should feel calm, modern, and immediately accessible. Ko-fi is known for its soft visual language, minimal clutter, and clear user guidance — and those principles should carry over into WikiLearn.

🧑‍🎨 Visual Style:
Color Palette:

Base tones: Beige, soft ivory, light sand, pastel cream

Accent colors: Muted coral, dusty rose, warm gold, light sky blue (used sparingly for CTAs or highlights)

Text: Mostly dark gray (#333) or warm black, never pure black/white contrast

Typography:

Use rounded, humanist sans-serif fonts (like Inter, Nunito, or Ko-fi’s custom font style)

Maintain clear visual hierarchy:

H1: Bold and warm

H2/H3: Soft and smaller but still readable

Body text: Comfortable line height, 16–18px font size on mobile

Shadows and Depth:

Use subtle shadows (10–15% opacity) for cards and modals to suggest layers without distraction

Rounded corners everywhere (preferably border-radius: 1.25rem for cards and buttons)

🖼️ Component Design:
1. Homepage Layout
Header with WikiLearn logo in soft gold or coral tone

Hero section with a search bar and optional background vector illustration (faint, abstract shapes like waves or circles)

Below that, a grid of soft category buttons (rounded rectangles with icons):

Examples: 🧪 Science, 🎨 Art, 🏛️ History, 💼 Business

When hovered/tapped: gentle elevation and color fade

2. Category / Subcategory Browsing
Display cards in infinite scroll

Card components include:

A Wikipedia image (rounded corners, soft border or drop shadow)

Short summary from Wikipedia (2–3 lines, light serif or soft sans-serif font)

A large and friendly “Learn” button (uses animated hover glow, Ko-fi style)

Optional toggle switch “Show articles only with image”:

Styled like Ko-fi donation toggles: pill shape, soft shadow, smooth animation

3. Lesson & Summary Page
Top section:

Breadcrumb navigation (Home > Art > Modern Art > Article Title)

Language level selector (radio buttons or dropdown, styled with soft cards)

Below:

Summary box (rounded, slightly elevated, calm background)

Lesson content area with icons for:

📘 Vocabulary

🧠 Grammar

📝 Extra Exercises

✏️ "Create Lesson" (primary CTA — use Ko-fi style animated button)

Premium warning (if needed): soft yellow or coral box with upgrade button, never aggressive or intrusive

🔔 Interactive Details (Ko-fi-like microinteractions):
All buttons should give instant visual feedback: ripple effect or color shift

Transitions: smooth fades/slides (250–400ms) between views, no jarring animations

Use soft sound cues (optional) for button presses or navigation like in cozy productivity tools

Floating “Help” or “? Tips” button in bottom-right corner — styled like Ko-fi’s support bubble

📱 Mobile First:
One-column layout

Sticky bottom navbar with icons: Home, Categories, History, Account

Large tappable cards with high contrast outlines and plenty of whitespace

Swipe gestures to navigate lessons and flashcards (inspired by cozy mobile apps)

🤍 Emotional Tone:
WikiLearn should feel like a safe, calm learning space.

It’s not a strict education app; it’s a cozy tool to explore knowledge, especially for people learning languages.

Think of it like a digital cafe for your brain — soft, intuitive, welcoming.
